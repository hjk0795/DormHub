Break a rule → get demerit points. Points add up and have serious consequences:
⚠️ Warning issued−10 pts
🚪 Eviction + banned next semester−15 pts
By violation type:
🚫Safety & order violations
→ Eviction + permanent ban (can never return)
⚠️Resident obligation violations
→ Eviction + banned next semester

🍺 Drinking is BANNED
Even empty bottles count as evidence!
−10 pts
You don't need to be caught drinking. If any trace is found — bottles, cans, smell — it counts as drinking.
🍷−10 demerit points — empty bottles, cans, or any sign of alcohol in your room
💡 Tip: Don't even store alcohol "for later." If found during inspection, it's −10 points.

Smoking

🚬 ABSOLUTELY NO SMOKING
In rooms, hallways, AND staircases!
−10 pts
Smoking of any kind is prohibited everywhere inside the building — rooms, hallways, staircases. This includes:
🚬Cigarettes, e-cigarettes, vapes, IQOS, heated tobacco, and all other smoking devices
🚨Fire sensors are extremely sensitive — smoking triggers the fire alarm for the entire building
💥DANGER: Some sections have gas pipes installed — smoking near them creates an explosion risk
⚖️Smoking violations may result in eviction + legal action (고발)
❌ Throwing cigarette butts on staircases or putting them out on walls/floors is also prohibited. Traces (smell, butts, smoke) count as evidence.
✅ Please smoke OUTSIDE the building only.

−10 pts

Utility Bills

💡If your electricity, gas, or water bill exceeds the building average by more than 10%, you must pay the excess amount

Move-out & Refunds

Voluntary move-out is not allowed for the first 30 days, except for special circumstances such as returning to your home country.
After 30 days — voluntary move-out:
Voluntary move-out penalty14 days fee
Forced eviction penalty30 days fee
Less than 30 days remaining
(20 days during vacation)No refund
Refund = Total paid minus penalty, then minus daily fee × days used up to your move-out date.
When moving out: return all issued items, get your room inspected, and compensate for any damage or missing items.

Fire Safety Law:
No Items in Hallways!

🚨 First offense fine: ₩1,000,000
(Korean Fire Safety Act)
By law, it is strictly prohibited to place any objects in the following areas:
🚲Hallways — no bicycles, boxes, shoe racks, etc.
🪴Staircases — no flower pots, furniture, bags, etc.
🚪Emergency exits — must be completely clear at all times
This is not just a dormitory rule — it is Korean law. Anyone can report it, and the fine starts at ₩1,000,000 (approx. $750) for the first offense.
Keep all personal items inside your room. No exceptions.
